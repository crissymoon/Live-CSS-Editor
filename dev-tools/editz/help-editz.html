Welcome to Editz!

Editz is a simple, easy-to-use editor that helps you create resumes, cover letters, and calendars right in your web browser. No complicated software needed - just click, type, and create!

Getting Started

First time using Editz? Click on any template from the "Templates" menu, then click the "Clear" button to start fresh and begin editing!

How Editz Works:

  • Auto-Save: Your work saves automatically as you type - no need to worry about losing your work!
  • Templates: Use ready-made resume and cover letter templates to get started fast
  • Easy Editing: Click anywhere in the white box and start typing
  • Save and Share: Save your work as PDF, Word, or HTML files

How to Resize Pictures

After you add a picture using the "Img" button, you can make it bigger or smaller by clicking on it!

Easy Picture Resizing:

  1. Small Size (First Click): When you first add a picture, it starts small. This is perfect for small logos or icons.
    Small size - good for logos and small images
  2. Medium Size (Second Click): Click the picture once, and it grows to medium size. Great for most photos!
    Medium size - perfect for most pictures
  3. Large Size (Third Click): Click the picture again, and it gets really big. Use this when you want the picture to stand out!
    Large size - makes your picture the main focus
Keep clicking to go back to small! The sizes loop around: small to medium to large to small again. Click as many times as you need to get the perfect size!

Editing Resume Skills (Important!)

When you use a resume template, you'll see a "Skills" section with example skills. Here's how to change them to YOUR skills:

Three Easy Ways to Edit Skills:

  1. Click and Highlight, Then Delete: Click on a skill box, highlight the text, and press the Delete key to remove it completely.
    Step 1: Highlight the skill and press Delete
  2. Backspace All Characters: Click inside a skill box and press Backspace until all the text is gone. The box will disappear.
    Step 2: Backspace to remove all text in the skill box
  3. Press Enter to Add New Skills: Click at the end of a skill and press Enter to create a new skill box. Then type your new skill!
    Step 3: Press Enter to add a new skill box
You can add as many skills as you want! Just keep pressing Enter to create new skill boxes.

Undo/Redo: Go backward or forward through your changes (like an eraser and un-eraser!)
Clear Format: Removes all formatting (colors, bold, etc.) from the text you select

How to Save as PDF

You can save your resume or document as a PDF using your web browser's built-in print feature. Here's how:

Step 1: Open the Print Menu

  • Windows: Press Ctrl + P
  • Mac: Press Cmd + P
  • Or: Right-click anywhere on the page and select "Print"

Step 2: Choose "Save as PDF"

  • In the print dialog, look for the Destination or Printer dropdown
  • Select "Save as PDF" or "Microsoft Print to PDF"
  • Do NOT select a regular printer

Step 3: Adjust Settings (Optional)

  • Layout: Choose Portrait or Landscape
  • Pages: Select "All" to save everything
  • Scale: Try 80% to 100% to fit your content nicely
  • Margins: Default or None both work well

Step 4: Save Your PDF

  • Click the "Save" or "Print" button
  • Choose where to save the file on your computer
  • Give it a name like "my-resume.pdf"
  • Click "Save"
Best Settings for Resumes: Use Line Spacing = 1.5, Font Size = Medium or Large, and when printing to PDF set Scale = 80% to fit everything on one page nicely.

Helpful Tips

To start fresh: Click a template, then click "Clear" to remove the example text and start typing your own information.
You can change how big your pictures are! Just click on any picture you add, and it will grow bigger each time you click. Keep clicking to cycle through small, medium, and large sizes!
The emoji button is in the toolbar, but Windows users can also press the Windows Key + Period (.) to open Windows' emoji picker!
Save your work often! Use "Save as Editz (HTML)" to keep a backup copy you can open and edit again later. HTML files are small and work on any computer!

Keyboard Shortcuts

  • Ctrl+Z (or Cmd+Z on Mac): Undo your last change
  • Ctrl+Y (or Cmd+Y on Mac): Redo a change you undid
  • Windows Key + . (period): Open emoji picker (Windows only)

Special Characters (Windows Computers):

Hold down the Alt key and type these numbers on your number pad (the numbers on the right side of your keyboard):

  • Alt + 0151: Long dash for emphasis
  • Alt + 26: Right arrow
  • Alt + 27: Left arrow
  • Alt + 0169: Copyright symbol
  • Alt + 0153: Trademark symbol

Special Characters (Mac Computers):

Hold down the Option key (sometimes labeled Alt) and press these keys:

  • Option + Shift + - (minus): Long dash for emphasis
  • Option + G: Copyright symbol
  • Option + 2: Trademark symbol
  • Control + Command + Space: Opens emoji and symbols picker (includes arrows and many more!)
Mac users: The emoji picker (Control + Command + Space) is the easiest way to find special symbols, arrows, and emojis. Just type what you are looking for, like "arrow" or "copyright"!
